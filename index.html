thepoisonproject

To structure and execute from an instinctive practice, where shapes answer to aesthetic whims, implies a responsibility to avoid contempt towards technicalities. Breaking up from the constant intention of design to reinterpretation of the known environment, a graphic universe is presented instead. one that behaves, acts and it is perceived in a specific language. thepoisonproject opens a path for experimentation, and breaks away from the traditional practices, to print using obsessive shapes, imprecise geometries and high visual contrast; an atypical visual universe.

Cinnabon Colombia's service and packaging design consultancy project for its new "On The Go" concept. In charge and led by Mayck Cuellar, Luisa Donado, Audrey Hirelle and Camila Gutierrez.

The packaging was designed to seal the product from the outside, that allows the vapor to conserve the heat inside. It also allows the smell to be spelled when opening the packaging, because the smell always get lost with the traditional package.

To allow other products to take advantage of the design features, there will be the possibility of assembling the beverage packaging along with the food package, depending on what the customer requests.

It also includes a small saw, plasticised, so that the user can unwind the cinnamon roll and have the possibility to cut it using only one hand.

A part of the package will allow one or two hot drinks to be loaded, and a steam is used to heat the product. The system allows the drinks to be fixed to the center of the package, widening the opening in the center of the box, so that it can be transported in a safe manner and by gravity action will always remain in the same place. It also allows the person to take the cup, drink and then put it back in place, allowing consumption on the road.

Manifestaciones

Interactive installation that responds to an exploration on the physical qualities of light as a resource to project the effects of sound over water.

The viewer interacts with the scene causing subtle changes in the sound that immerses him in a light experience.

The development of this project is the result of a process of experimentation and exploration of form through light and its physical qualities. It arises from my interest in the effects produced by light and its ability to intervene in our perception of the environment.

For this project the cymatics play a fundamental role, the light projections are configured in the space of the experience and reacts to alterations on the surface of the water. The vibrations responsible for these changes are the result of the viewer's position in space, the system will know that because of several movement sensors around the space.

The design in this case permeates all aspects of the installation offering an aesthetic proposal according to the fundamentals applied to the interaction, graphic composition and product development.

VR/Tactile interfaces for architects

This project included the evaluation, study and design of big tactile and VR interfaces initially created by engineers and architects. I worked close to the engineering team to propose new design interfaces delivered to construction companies across the country, the interfaces followed several UX tecniques, UI patterns and usability heuristics to deliver robust interfaces often used by the general public. Service blueprints and workflows were created in realtimeboard, mockups in Adobe XD and Illustrator, and the development was made by engineers in web/Unreal engines.
